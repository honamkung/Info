Research during PhD

During my PhD, I have mainly investigated molecular and neural circuit mechanisms of higher-order brain functions that are frequently impaired across a wide array of psychiatric and neurological disorders.

The miR-124-AMPAR pathway connects polygenic risks with behavioral changes shared between schizophrenia and bipolar disorder

Ho Namkung

, Hiroshi Yukitake, Daisuke Fukudome, Brian J. Lee, Gianluca Ursini, Shravika Lam, Suvarnambiga Kannan, Atsushi Saito, Minae Niwa, Kamal Sharma, Peter Zandi, Hanna Jaaro-Peled, Koko Ishizuka, Nilanjan Chatterjee, Richard Huganir, Akira Sawa
bioRxiv (2021)

pdf

We showed that the polygenic risks shared between schizophrenia and bipolar disorder converge on a common neurobiological pathway (miR-124-AMPAR pathway) that in turn influences the behavioral dimensions shared between these two psychotic disorders.

The anterior insular cortex associates temporally discontiguous stimuli during threat learning

Ho Namkung

, Sedona Lockhart, Josephine de Chabot, Lauren Guttman, Imad Isehak, Hyung-Bae Kwon, Akira Sawa
bioRxiv (2021)

pdf

We identified that activity of anterior insular cortex layer 5 pyramidal neurons, which reciprocally interact with the basolateral amygdala, is required for associating temporally discontiguous stimuli, specifically during a time interval between them.
